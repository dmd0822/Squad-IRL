# A/B Test Analysis Grading Rubric

## Statistical Rigor (30 points)

- **Calculation Accuracy (15 pts)** - Are p-values, confidence intervals, and lift calculated correctly?
- **Sample Size Validation (10 pts)** - Is minimum sample size verified for adequate statistical power?
- **Test Validity Checks (5 pts)** - Are implementation issues, novelty effects, or data quality flagged?

## Interpretation Quality (25 points)

- **Statistical vs. Practical (10 pts)** - Is practical significance (effect size) distinguished from statistical?
- **Business Impact (10 pts)** - Is result translated to projected revenue/conversion/engagement impact?
- **Segment Insights (5 pts)** - Are meaningful segment differences identified and explained?

## Recommendation Clarity (20 points)

- **Clear Decision (12 pts)** - Is there a definitive ship/don't ship/test longer recommendation?
- **Justification (8 pts)** - Is reasoning for decision well-explained with evidence?

## Completeness (15 points)

- **Risk Assessment (8 pts)** - Are potential downsides or edge cases identified?
- **Next Steps (7 pts)** - Are follow-up tests or iterations suggested?

## Communication (10 points)

- **Clarity (5 pts)** - Are statistical concepts explained in business-friendly language?
- **Visual/Tabular (5 pts)** - Are results presented clearly with appropriate formatting?

## Scoring Guidelines

- **90-100**: Rigorous analysis that drives confident decisions
- **80-89**: Sound statistical analysis with clear recommendation
- **70-79**: Correct calculations but weak interpretation or business impact
- **60-69**: Statistical errors or insufficient validation
- **Below 60**: Unreliable analysis—would lead to poor decisions
