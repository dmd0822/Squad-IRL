# Negotiation Script Update Guidance

## Update Strategy

When feedback indicates issues:

1. **Add Market Data** - Include specific benchmarks, salary ranges, or comparable deals
2. **Strengthen Opening** - Make opening statement more confident with better justification
3. **Expand Objections** - Add more objection scenarios with scripted responses
4. **Identify Alternatives** - List more non-monetary negotiables beyond primary ask
5. **Clarify Range** - Better justify ideal, target, and walk-away numbers

## Focus Areas

- **If scored low on Preparation**: Add specific market data sources and numbers
- **If scored low on Script Quality**: Strengthen opening; add more objection responses
- **If scored low on Strategic Depth**: Add non-monetary alternatives; improve anchoring guidance
- **If scored low on Relationship**: Soften adversarial language; add collaborative phrases

## Update Rules

- Every number should have supporting data: "According to [source], the range is..."
- Add at least 6 common objections with specific scripted responses
- Include 3-5 non-monetary alternatives (equity, title, PTO, flexibility, etc.)
- Opening script should be one clear statement, not a paragraph
- Walk-away script should be gracious, not bitter
- Add specific "silence moments": "After stating your number, wait for them to respond"

## Quality Check

Before resubmitting:
- Can the user cite specific market data for their ask?
- Are there scripted responses for "that's too high" and "that's our final offer"?
- Are non-monetary alternatives included?
- Does the tone stay collaborative throughout?
- Would this prep give someone confidence going into the negotiation?
