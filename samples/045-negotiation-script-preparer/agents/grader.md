# Negotiation Script Grading Rubric

## Preparation & Research (30 points)

- **Market Data (15 pts)** - Are numbers supported by relevant market research or comps?
- **Range Strategy (10 pts)** - Are ideal, target, and walk-away numbers clearly defined and realistic?
- **Context Understanding (5 pts)** - Does script reflect understanding of leverage and situation?

## Script Quality (25 points)

- **Opening Confidence (10 pts)** - Is opening statement confident, clear, and well-justified?
- **Objection Responses (15 pts)** - Are 5-8 common objections anticipated with effective responses?

## Strategic Depth (20 points)

- **Non-Monetary Alternatives (10 pts)** - Are creative alternatives identified beyond the primary ask?
- **Silence & Timing (5 pts)** - Is there guidance on when to pause, pivot, or ask questions?
- **Anchoring Strategy (5 pts)** - Is there clear guidance on when to name a number vs. let them offer?

## Relationship Preservation (15 points)

- **Collaborative Tone (10 pts)** - Does script maintain partnership tone, not adversarial?
- **Walk-Away Grace (5 pts)** - Is there a dignified exit if negotiation doesn't meet minimums?

## Confidence Building (10 points)

- **Actionability (5 pts)** - Can the user actually deliver these scripts with confidence?
- **Evidence Backup (5 pts)** - Are claims and asks backed by evidence the user can cite?

## Scoring Guidelines

- **90-100**: Professional negotiation prep that builds confidence and gets results
- **80-89**: Solid preparation with good data and scripts
- **70-79**: Useful but missing key objections or insufficient market data
- **60-69**: Generic advice without specific scripts or weak anchoring
- **Below 60**: Unprepared—lacks data, strategy, or confidence-building elements
