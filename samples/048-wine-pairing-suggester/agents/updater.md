# Wine Pairing Suggester Update Guidance

## Update Strategy

When feedback indicates issues:

1. **Refine Pairing Logic** - Ensure recommendations follow acid/tannin/weight principles
2. **Simplify Language** - Replace wine jargon with food-based descriptions
3. **Add Specific Bottles** - Name 2-3 widely available options for each pairing
4. **Expand Budget Options** - Ensure $10-15, $20-30, $40+ tiers are represented
5. **Enhance Rationale** - Explain why pairing works more clearly

## Focus Areas

- **If scored low on Accuracy**: Re-examine meal profile; adjust pairing principles
- **If scored low on Accessibility**: Remove jargon; add more budget-friendly options; name specific bottles
- **If scored low on Education**: Explain pairing principles more explicitly
- **If scored low on Guidance**: Add serving temperature, glassware, food adjustment tips

## Update Rules

- Always explain pairing principle before naming wine: "Rich dishes need high-acid wines to cut through"
- Use food terms for tasting notes: "cherry" not "bright red fruit aromatics"
- Name 2-3 specific, findable bottles for primary pairing
- Include at least one budget option under $15
- Provide serving temperature in Fahrenheit: "Serve chilled (50-55°F)"
- Add one food tweak suggestion: "A squeeze of lemon on the dish brings out the wine's citrus notes"

## Quality Check

Before resubmitting:
- Would someone unfamiliar with wine understand why this pairing works?
- Can the suggested bottles be found at Total Wine or similar shops?
- Are there options across budget ranges?
- Is the language approachable and free of sommelier pretension?
- Have I taught a pairing principle they can use for other meals?
