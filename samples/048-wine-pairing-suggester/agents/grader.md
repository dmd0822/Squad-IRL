# Wine Pairing Suggester Grading Rubric

## Pairing Accuracy (30 points)

- **Principle Soundness (15 pts)** - Do pairings follow established somm principles (acid, tannin, weight)?
- **Meal Analysis (10 pts)** - Are dominant flavors and richness correctly identified?
- **Rationale Clarity (5 pts)** - Is it clear why each pairing works (complement/contrast)?

## Accessibility (25 points)

- **Language (12 pts)** - Are tasting notes and explanations free of pretentious jargon?
- **Bottle Availability (8 pts)** - Are suggested bottles findable in decent wine shops?
- **Budget Range (5 pts)** - Are options provided across $10-15, $20-30, $40+ tiers?

## Education Value (20 points)

- **Principle Teaching (10 pts)** - Do readers learn pairing principles they can apply elsewhere?
- **Tasting Notes (10 pts)** - Are flavor profiles described in helpful, relatable terms?

## Practical Guidance (15 points)

- **Serving Details (8 pts)** - Are temperature, glassware, and timing included?
- **Food Adjustments (7 pts)** - Are minor meal tweaks suggested to enhance pairing?

## Comprehensiveness (10 points)

- **Alternatives (5 pts)** - Are non-wine options (beer, cocktails) suggested?
- **Variety (5 pts)** - Are 2-3 alternatives provided beyond primary pairing?

## Scoring Guidelines

- **90-100**: Sommelier-grade pairing advice that educates and delights
- **80-89**: Solid pairings with good rationale and accessibility
- **70-79**: Correct pairings but overly technical or limited options
- **60-69**: Weak pairing logic or pretentious language
- **Below 60**: Incorrect pairing principles or unhelpful suggestions
