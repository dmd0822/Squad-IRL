# Parent Email Drafter Specification

## Goal
Draft professional, empathetic emails to parents that address concerns, share information, or request support while building strong home-school partnerships.

## Requirements

1. **Purpose Clarity** - Email purpose is clear in subject line and first sentence
2. **Positive Framing** - Start with strengths/positives even when addressing concerns
3. **Specific Examples** - Use concrete observations rather than generalizations
4. **Collaborative Tone** - Position teacher and parent as partners, not adversaries
5. **Action Items** - Include clear next steps for both teacher and parent
6. **Professional Boundaries** - Maintain appropriate teacher-parent relationship boundaries
7. **Empathy & Respect** - Acknowledge parent perspective and family circumstances
8. **Documentation-Ready** - Language is appropriate for potential inclusion in student records
9. **Response Facilitation** - Make it easy for parents to respond with needed information
10. **Cultural Sensitivity** - Respectful of diverse family structures and communication preferences

## Output Format

```markdown
**Subject:** [Clear, non-alarming subject line]

Dear [Parent Name],

[Opening: positive observation or appreciation]

[Body: specific information, concern, or request with concrete examples]

[Partnership statement: how we can work together]

[Action items: what I'll do + what I'm hoping you can do]

[Closing: available for questions + appreciation]

Best regards,
[Teacher Name]
```

## Quality Criteria

- **Tone**: Warm and professional, never defensive or accusatory
- **Clarity**: Parents immediately understand purpose and any actions needed
- **Partnership**: Language invites collaboration rather than demands compliance
- **Specificity**: Concrete examples rather than vague characterizations
- **Professionalism**: Appropriate for documentation while remaining human and empathetic
