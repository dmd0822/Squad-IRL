# Parent Email Grading Rubric

## Tone & Partnership (30 points)

- **Positive Opening (10 pts)** - Does email start with genuine strength/positive observation?
- **Collaborative Language (12 pts)** - Is parent positioned as partner rather than problem?
- **Empathy (8 pts)** - Does email acknowledge parent perspective and circumstances?

## Clarity & Specificity (25 points)

- **Purpose Clarity (8 pts)** - Is email purpose immediately clear from subject and opening?
- **Concrete Examples (10 pts)** - Are specific observations provided rather than generalizations?
- **Action Items (7 pts)** - Are next steps clear for both teacher and parent?

## Professionalism (20 points)

- **Documentation-Ready (10 pts)** - Is language appropriate for student records if needed?
- **Boundaries (5 pts)** - Does email maintain professional teacher-parent relationship?
- **Cultural Sensitivity (5 pts)** - Is language respectful of diverse family situations?

## Effectiveness (15 points)

- **Response Facilitation (8 pts)** - Is it easy for parent to respond or take action?
- **Solution Focus (7 pts)** - Are solutions/approaches offered, not just problems?

## Structure & Brevity (10 points)

- **Organization (5 pts)** - Is email logically structured and easy to follow?
- **Conciseness (5 pts)** - Is message conveyed efficiently without unnecessary length?

## Scoring Guidelines

- **90-100**: Master teacher communication that strengthens partnerships
- **80-89**: Professional and effective parent communication
- **70-79**: Gets the message across but could be more collaborative or specific
- **60-69**: Potentially defensive, vague, or lacking partnership tone
- **Below 60**: Would damage parent relationship or inappropriate for documentation
