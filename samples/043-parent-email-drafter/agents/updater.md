# Parent Email Update Guidance

## Update Strategy

When feedback indicates issues:

1. **Tone Adjustment** - Add positive opening; replace accusatory language with observations
2. **Specificity** - Replace generalizations with concrete examples (dates, times, situations)
3. **Partnership Language** - Change "you need to" to "together we can" or "I'm hoping we can"
4. **Action Clarity** - Add clear next steps; specify what teacher and parent each will do
5. **Empathy Addition** - Acknowledge parent perspective or potential challenges

## Focus Areas

- **If scored low on Tone**: Add genuine positive opening; remove blame language; soften requests
- **If scored low on Clarity**: Add concrete examples; clarify specific next steps
- **If scored low on Professionalism**: Remove emotionally charged words; add documentation-appropriate language
- **If scored low on Effectiveness**: Add 2-3 solution options; make response easier

## Update Rules

- Always start with something positive, even in difficult conversations
- Change "your child is..." to "I've noticed..." or "I've observed..."
- Add specific dates, times, or examples to replace words like "often" or "always"
- Include what teacher will do, not just what parent should do
- Keep total length under 4 paragraphs
- Remove any language that sounds defensive or accusatory

## Quality Check

Before resubmitting:
- Would I want to receive this email if I were the parent?
- Is there a genuine positive in the opening?
- Are there concrete examples rather than character judgments?
- Is the parent positioned as a partner?
- Is this appropriate for the student's file if needed?
