# Specification: Vet Appointment Prep Tool

## Goal
Help pet owners prepare thoroughly for vet visits by organizing symptom logs, behavior changes, and questions to ensure nothing is forgotten and communication with vet is clear and efficient.

## Requirements
1. **Symptom Timeline**: Chronological log of symptoms with frequency and severity
2. **Behavior Changes**: Document appetite, energy, bathroom habits, social behavior
3. **Question Generation**: Suggest important questions based on symptoms
4. **Medical History**: Compile recent treatments, medications, supplements
5. **Trigger Identification**: Note potential causes or patterns (diet change, new food, stressors)
6. **Objective Metrics**: Measurable data (weight, water intake, vomiting frequency)
7. **Photo/Video Notes**: Remind to capture symptoms on camera if relevant
8. **Urgency Assessment**: Flag symptoms needing immediate vs. routine care
9. **Cost Discussion**: Prep questions about treatment costs and options
10. **Follow-Up Planning**: Questions about next steps, monitoring, red flags

## Input Format
- Pet details (species, breed, age, weight)
- Current symptoms or concerns
- Symptom duration and changes over time
- Current medications and diet
- Recent changes in routine or environment
- Previous vet visit notes

## Output Format
Vet visit prep document with:
- Symptom summary for vet (chronological)
- Behavior change checklist
- Objective measurements and data
- List of current medications/supplements
- Potential trigger factors
- Prioritized question list
- Important notes to mention
- Photos/videos to show
- Cost discussion questions
- Follow-up questions for next steps

## Quality Criteria
- Comprehensive without overwhelming vet
- Clear, factual symptom descriptions
- Questions are relevant and well-organized
- Helps owner be pet's best advocate
- Reduces risk of forgetting important details
